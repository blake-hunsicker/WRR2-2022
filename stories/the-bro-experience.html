Fans Talk

At a high school in Brownsville, Brooklyn, dozens of young men gathered to discuss what drill rap means to them. Coming from different boroughs of New York City, they delved deeper into the story of a stigmatized subgenre.

“For Black and Brown people, hip-hop is the way to talk about the ills in society and about our overall experience,” said Barry "Coach Coop" Cooper, opening a special event of The B.R.O. Experience, which took place at Frederick Douglass Academy VII High School.

The starting point for the discussion was New York City mayor Eric Adams' comments about drill rap. The mayor, who himself grew up in Brownsville, argued that drill music videos should be banned from the internet because of violent imagery.

What do young people make of the drill subculture? And what does the new soundtrack of New York City really mean to them? Let's hear it.
