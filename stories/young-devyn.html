In the Hood and on Disney

Young Devyn is a rising New York rapper who started to experiment with drill by chance. She decided to post a freestyle video on social media. The raw footage eventually became “Straight to It”, her top-performing song with more than 650,000 views on YouTube. While drill is often associated with violence, Devyn tries a different approach when working in the subgenre. For instance, she doesn't curse.

“If you listen to my song, you might be like: 'Damn, she just said something crazy!' but I didn't curse,” she said. “I'm trying to be in the hood and on Disney.”

Coming from a Trinidadian family, Devyn started her career singing Soca - a popular Caribbean style of music. She was only 7 when she first performed at a school event to celebrate Caribbean heritage in New York, surprising even her mom, who was amazed by her confidence on stage. After transitioning to a different genre, her cultural background is still an important part of who she is on stage.

“Every time you hear me rap, I always say 'Brooklyn/Trinidad,'” said Devyn. “So I always just make sure I represent them because they represented me first.”
