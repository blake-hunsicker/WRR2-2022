A Brooklyn Girl Calls the Shots

Shauna James reps East Flatbush everywhere she goes. Now a digital marketing manager at Atlantic Records, Shauna often attributes her success to her Brooklyn and Caribbean roots. She came up in the Brooklyn hip-hop scene while also constantly being surrounded by the sounds of Jamaican Reggae and Trinidadian Soca and Calypso. Music was always a centerpoint of her life, and she adds a bit of Caribbean flair to everything she does.

Shauna's understanding of Brooklyn hip-hop goes beyond her appreciation of music. She grew up in the culture, and has grown up with some of the artists she works with.

“Everybody knows each other because, before this music stuff, everyone was outside. When you look at the Fivio [Foreign]s, Pop Smokes, Sheff Gs and all of them, like, I know people who grew up with them,” Shauna said. “A lot of people from the hood, they're doing big things.”

In an industry where many drill artists struggle with incarceration and violence, Shauna strives to provide her artists with a support system that they may not have elsewhere. Shauna doesn't just market artists, she gets them. Her empathy and understanding of the hardships artists face set her apart at Atlantic Records.

Shauna's success so far has proven that the girl she once thought was “too ghetto” is now a key player at one of hip-hop's major labels.
